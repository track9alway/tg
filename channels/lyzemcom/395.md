Jitsi and its main instance
Recently, Jitsi made authentication mandatory to access its excellent videoconferencing service. This choice, in our opinion, undermines user privacy.
To use Jitsi from the main instance, it is now necessary to have an account with Google, GitHub (Microsoft) or Facebook. This list may change at a later date, according to what was
communicated
by the executives of 8x8, the company that owns the instance in question.
Although one can understand the motivations behind this choice, Lyzem distances itself from it, since it is indisputable that the service offered is no longer as anonymous as before. The privacy to which they had accustomed us is no longer there: now it is identified who activates the service. It cannot be ruled out that in the near future the service may be inhibited for those who are deemed unwelcome.
Since we are not fans of these rules, and even less of authentication via Google, Microsoft, Facebook, Apple, etc., we have decided to remove their instance from
@mLyzembot
and replace it with other Jitsi instances scattered around the world, which guarantee access to the service without authentication.
